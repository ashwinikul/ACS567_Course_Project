Have fun taking the the MBTI test and getting to know yourself better!

MBTI Benifit

The MBTI helps individuals see the differences in their innate personalities: how they perceive the world and how they prefer to act.

MBTI Development

The Myers-Briggs inventory is based on Carl Jung's theory of types. It was introduced in 1943 by Katharine C. Myers Briggs and Isabel Briggs.

Many Companies Use MBTI

Well know companies include: McKinsey & Company, the CIA, the Department of State, and 89 of the Fortune 100 companies.

50 Million People Have Taken The MBTI Test Since 1962

More than 10,000 companies, 2,500 colleges and universities and 200 government agencies in the United States use the test.

About MBTI

What is MBTI?

The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator instrument is the most administered and most recognized personality type tool in the world. Based on the model by Carl Jung and made manifest by Isabel Myers, the MBTI tool provides a framework for understanding one's way of both collecting information and making decisions to allow for maximizing personal and interpersonal effectiveness.

MBTI Benefits

After more than 50 years of development, the MBTI instrument continues to be one of the leading personality assessment tools for:

  • Understanding individual differences.
  • Team-building - uncovering new ways to work and interact with others.
  • Executive coaching and management training.
  • Performance management - improving individual and team performance.
  • Retaining top talent.
  • Developing effective leadership skills.
  • Reducing workplace conflict and enhancing teamwork.
  • Career assessment, career development and mid-career change.
